# The psychology of coming back — rewards, dopamine & the right level of abstraction

How to leverage dopamine and other human inclinations so people **return daily**
and **finish their tasks** — and exactly **what to show them, and how much**, to
keep them motivated without overwhelming or manipulating them. Researched
2026-06-25. This is the _strategy_ doc; `research/01-competitive-landscape.md` is
the _what-others-do_ doc, and `docs/UI_PRACTICES.md` is the _visual system_.

> **The thesis (PRD §1, §retention):** dhikr is repetitive habit-maintenance —
> the exact case where dopamine mechanics work — but they only _last_ when
> anchored in real group accountability + forgiveness. So we lean into earned
> dopamine, and deliberately stay **white-hat**: motivate without shaming,
> reward without addicting. North Star = 70%+ group consistency over 90 days.

---

## Part A — The human inclinations we can leverage

Eleven well-evidenced mechanisms, each with the one-line _why_ and the Cetele
lever it maps to.

| #   | Mechanism                               | What the science says                                                                                                                                                                | Cetele lever                                                                                                               |
| --- | --------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1   | **Dopamine = anticipation, not reward** | Dopamine spikes when the brain _expects_ a reward; it encodes a **reward-prediction error** — the bigger the positive surprise, the bigger the signal and the stronger the learning. | Make the _approach_ to a closed ring feel good (animation builds as you near target), not just the completion.             |
| 2   | **Variable-ratio reward** (Skinner)     | Unpredictable rewards are the **most powerful reinforcement schedule** — variability creates a "hunting" state in the wanting/seeking system.                                        | **Surprise milestone reveals** (CET-10): an occasional dua / animation at unpredictable moments, not every time.           |
| 3   | **Loss aversion** (Kahneman & Tversky)  | Losing feels **~2× as intense** as gaining the same thing. A streak is something you _own_ and fear losing.                                                                          | The streak + the live group goal you'd "let the group down" by missing. **But framed gently** (see ethics).                |
| 4   | **Goal-gradient effect**                | Effort _accelerates_ as people near a goal; progress bars trigger it.                                                                                                                | Rings, the group counter nearing 100%, "3 taps from done" nudges.                                                          |
| 5   | **Zeigarnik effect**                    | People remember and feel pulled back to **unfinished** tasks.                                                                                                                        | Show today's _open_ rings prominently; a reminder that names what's left.                                                  |
| 6   | **Endowed-progress effect**             | Artificial early progress toward a goal **increases completion** (loyalty cards started at 2/10 beat 0/8).                                                                           | Onboarding starts you "already part-way" — a pre-filled first day / group goal already moving.                             |
| 7   | **Fresh-start effect**                  | Temporal landmarks (Monday, 1st, new month) make people **more likely to start/restart**.                                                                                            | Re-engage lapsed users on Hijri new month, Ramadan, Mondays; offer a clean "new week" framing.                             |
| 8   | **Self-Determination Theory**           | Sustained motivation needs **autonomy + competence + relatedness**. Controlling "do X to get Y" rewards _undermine_ intrinsic interest.                                              | Autonomy: choose your tasks/targets. Competence: visible progress. Relatedness: the group (our strongest card).            |
| 9   | **Fogg Behavior Model** (B=MAP)         | Behavior = Motivation × Ability × Prompt, at one moment. **Raising ability (lowering friction) is more reliable than raising motivation.**                                           | Keep logging a 1-tap act; put the prompt at the right moment; never depend on willpower.                                   |
| 10  | **Hook Model** (Eyal)                   | Habits form via **Trigger → Action → Variable reward → Investment**, looped. Investment (your data, your group) raises switching cost.                                               | Notification → tap-count → (sometimes) surprise → your streak/history/group deepen.                                        |
| 11  | **Octalysis white vs black hat** (Chou) | White-hat drives (meaning, accomplishment, ownership) feel good and last; black-hat drives (urgency, unpredictability, loss) drive action but **burn users out** if relied on.       | Lead white-hat (meaning of dhikr, group belonging, mastery); use black-hat (streak loss, scarcity) _sparingly and gently_. |

**The synthesis:** Cetele already owns the rare, durable drives — **relatedness**
(a real group), **meaning** (worship), **forgiveness**. The opportunity is to add
the _spark_ layer — anticipation, variable reward, goal-gradient nudges, and a
trigger — **without** tipping into the burn-out black-hat zone.

## Part B — Cetele's reward system (the reward ladder)

Rewards should **escalate, not saturate** (hedonic adaptation makes constant
fireworks meaningless), and mix **fixed** rewards (reliable, build the habit) with
**variable** ones (unpredictable, create the pull). Map:

| Reward                                             | Trigger                        | Schedule              | Drive / need it serves              | Status               |
| -------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------ | --------------------- | ----------------------------------- | -------------------- |
| Tap feedback (haptic + count pop)                  | every tap                      | **fixed**             | competence, instant feedback (Fogg) | ✅ live              |
| Ring fills + closes (green)                        | reaching a task target         | **fixed**             | goal-gradient, completion           | ✅ live              |
| Streak +1                                          | a fully complete day           | **fixed**             | loss aversion, identity             | ✅ live              |
| Streak freeze used                                 | a missed day (1 in hand)       | safety net            | forgiveness (anti-shame)            | ✅ live              |
| Live group counter ticks                           | anyone in the group taps       | **fixed**, ambient    | relatedness, social proof           | ✅ live              |
| **Surprise milestone reveal** (dua/animation)      | random, near real milestones   | **variable** ⚡       | dopamine/anticipation (strongest)   | 🟡 CET-10 — **ship** |
| **Peer reaction** (one-tap dua/"barakAllahu feek") | a peer closes their rings      | **variable** (social) | relatedness                         | ❌ propose           |
| **Monthly/▸ Hijri-month award badge**              | sustained consistency          | **fixed** at landmark | accomplishment, fresh-start         | ❌ propose           |
| **Group "garden" growth**                          | collective goal met repeatedly | **fixed**, cumulative | ownership, a thing you protect      | ❌ propose           |
| **Milestone streaks** (7/30/100-day reveals)       | crossing a streak landmark     | **fixed** at landmark | escalating accomplishment           | ❌ propose           |

**Reward-design rules:**

1. **Reliable for habit, variable for pull.** Fixed rewards (ring close, streak)
   build the routine; **one** variable reward (the surprise dua) supplies the
   dopamine hunt. Don't make core progress feel random.
2. **Escalate.** Same-size win → same-size celebration; reserve the big burst for
   the big finish (first 7-day streak, group's first 90-day goal).
3. **Earned, never a slot machine.** Variable rewards fire at _real_ achievements
   (a true milestone), with bounded surprise — sustainable habit, not dependence.
4. **Rewards point back to meaning.** The surprise is a **dua / ayah / reflection**
   — the reward reinforces _why they're here_, not just a confetti hit.

## Part C — The perfect level of abstraction (what to show, and how much)

The core question: **show enough to motivate, abstract enough not to overwhelm or
shame.** The research is clear — minimalist dashboards let people read trends
**~22% faster**; progress bars _raise_ motivation **without** adding cognitive
load; but more data under poor design causes overload that _distracts_. The tool
is **progressive disclosure**: lead with the one thing that matters, let detail be
opt-in.

### The motivation surface — three layers

```
GLANCE  (always visible, 1 number + 1 visual)   →  "Am I on track today?"
            open rings · today's streak · group goal %
DETAIL  (one tap deeper)                         →  "How am I doing over time?"
            the 90-day heatmap · 7/30/90 score · this week's board
RAW     (opt-in, rarely surfaced)                →  "Show me everything"
            exact counts/day, per-task tables, history export
```

### What to show vs. abstract vs. hide

| Signal                            | Treatment                                                                        | Why                                                                                           |
| --------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Today's open rings**            | **Show, front and centre**                                                       | Zeigarnik + goal-gradient: the unfinished thing pulls you to finish.                          |
| **Current streak**                | **Show**, but small & calm                                                       | Loss aversion works — but a giant red counter creates dread (black-hat).                      |
| **Group goal % today**            | **Show** (the hero)                                                              | Relatedness + social proof: the collective is our unique, durable driver.                     |
| **Consistency %**                 | **Abstract to a band**                                                           | "Steady · 83%" or a ring, not a stressful raw decimal. Don't make a number feel like a grade. |
| **The 90-day heatmap**            | **One tap deep**                                                                 | Rich, motivating _pattern_ — but not glanceable; lives on /progress.                          |
| **Exact per-task counts/history** | **Hide by default; opt-in**                                                      | RAW layer. Most users never need it; surfacing it adds load (minimalism reads 22% faster).    |
| **Where you rank vs others**      | **Soften** — small group, "you" highlighted, **never a global/permanent ladder** | Permanent leaderboards demotivate the bottom half (black-hat).                                |
| **A missed day**                  | **Neutral, not red**                                                             | Show as a lighter cell + "never miss twice" message; never alarm.                             |
| **"At-risk" streak**              | **Gentle prompt, not a threat**                                                  | Reframe as forgiveness, not "you're about to LOSE everything."                                |

### Abstraction rules to hold

1. **One headline per screen.** Each view answers _one_ question with one number +
   one visual. Everything else is progressive disclosure.
2. **Abstract numbers into meaning.** Prefer a labelled band / ring / word
   ("Steady", "On track", "✓ met") over a raw figure when the figure could read as
   a judgement. Keep raw precision for the opt-in RAW layer.
3. **Show progress, not just totals.** A bar/ring toward a target out-motivates a
   lifetime count (goal-gradient). Always frame against a reachable goal.
4. **Lead with the unfinished.** Surface what's _left today_ before what's done
   (Zeigarnik) — but pair with a clear, achievable path to close it.
5. **Hide complexity until asked.** Default to the GLANCE layer; never open a
   screen on a dense table. Minimalism = faster comprehension + lower load.
6. **Every motivating number has a calm version.** If a metric can shame on a bad
   day, design its low state to reassure, not punish.

## Part D — Ethical guardrails (why we stay white-hat)

Cetele is a worship tool; manipulation here is doubly wrong. Black-hat mechanics
(urgency, loss, unpredictability) _work_ but **burn users out and erode control**,
and dark patterns exploit the very people we're meant to serve.

- **No shame, ever.** "Never miss twice" forgiveness; a bad day is met with
  reassurance (Finch's lesson), not guilt.
- **No FOMO-red.** Red is errors only — never "your streak is dying!" urgency.
- **Easy, guilt-free exit.** Let people pause / take a break without punishment;
  ethical design assumes users sometimes step away.
- **No permanent/global leaderboard.** Keep competition small, winnable, and
  optional; rank softly within the real group.
- **Reward reinforces meaning.** Surprises are duas/reflections — pointing back to
  _why_, not engineered compulsion.
- **Measure the right thing.** Success = returns + closed rings + 90-day
  consistency, **not** time-in-app. We're not optimising for screen time.
